About Us

Adidas was founded by Adolf "Adi" Dassler, who made his first shoes in his mother's wash kitchen in Herzogenaurach, Germany after his return from World War I. In 1924, his brother Rudolf joined the business which became Gebrüder Dassler Schuhfabrik (Dassler Brothers Shoe Factory).

The brothers split up in 1948 due to a rift between them, with Rudolf forming a new firm that he called Ruda – from Rudolf Dassler, later rebranded Puma, while Adi formed a company formally registered as Adidas AG on 18 August 1949.

Adidas' logo is three stripes, which is used on the company's clothing and shoe designs as a marketing aid. The branding, which Adidas bought in 1952 from Finnish sports company Karhu Sports for two bottles of whiskey and the equivalent of 1600 euros, became so successful that Dassler described Adidas as "The three stripes company".
